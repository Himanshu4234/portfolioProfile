## Overview

**LiveLaw** is a premier legal news portal in India, providing real-time updates, court judgments, opinion pieces, and legal analysis. It is trusted by legal professionals, law students, and anyone interested in Indian legal developments. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}  
The site also offers a **subscription model** for premium access — subscribers get ad-free content, archives, judgment downloads, and exclusive legal insights. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Key Features

- **Real-Time Legal Reporting**  
  Covers breaking court news, Supreme Court / High Court judgments, and legal commentary.

- **Subscription-Based Premium Content**  
  Provides paid users with ad-free browsing, unlimited access to archives, downloadable judgments, and deeper editorial content. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

- **Structured Legal Articles**  
  Publishes in-depth legal analysis, columns, and explainer pieces on current laws, constitutional issues, and landmark judgments.

- **SEO & Semantic Content**  
  Uses structured metadata and SEO best practices to ensure high visibility and reach for legal news and analysis.

- **Accessibility & Performance**  
  Built to handle heavy content, with optimized performance for text-heavy pages, fast loading, and responsive design.

- **Mobile & App Presence**  
  Available on iOS / Android — users can download judgments, bookmark articles, and receive notifications. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Challenges & Learnings

- **Balancing Depth and Speed**: How to give users thorough legal analysis without slowing down page load.  
- **Performance on Archive Pages**: Loading heavy legal archives and judgments efficiently.  
- **Subscription UI Design**: Making premium features accessible and clear without disrupting user flow.  
- **Content Structure**: Designing reusable components for articles, judgments, and legal commentary.  
- **SEO for Legal Content**: Structuring content for discoverability and credibility with Google and legal researchers.
